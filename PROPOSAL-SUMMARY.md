# SONARA Proposal Summary

**Sounds of NASA: Research Activated** — NASA Science Activation Team Proposal

## Overview

SONARA capitalizes on sonification of space data to engage the public with heliophysics. Many heliophysics phenomena are invisible but produce waves in audible frequency ranges. Scientists already name these by sound (whistlers, lion roars, chorus). SONARA uses this to bridge science and public engagement.

## Three Goals

### Goal 1: Education & Communication
Use sound to communicate heliophysics concepts and engage the public in sonification-focused educational activities.

**Deliverables:**
- Narrated "Sounds of the Solar System" planetarium show (distributable to planetariums nationwide)
- Live musician performances using sonified data at planetariums with visualizations
- Interactive web-based storyboards where users explore sounds alongside data/phenomena
- Monthly public webinars on sonification advances
- Online sonification database / "clearing house" of NASA mission sounds

**Key details:**
- Multi-channel spatial audio (11.2 speaker system) powered by OpenSpace software
- 26 Summer Bridge Scholars pilot at CU Boulder
- 360-degree full-dome immersion experiences
- Storyboards tuned to each audience (K-12, university, citizen scientists, musicians)

**Data sources:** MMS, Parker Solar Probe, Van Allen Probes, THEMIS, Voyager, Cassini, Cluster

### Goal 2: Citizen Science
Increase participation in NASA heliophysics through citizen science using sonification.

**Deliverables:**
- Citizen science project incubator using sonified NASA datasets
- Web-based GUI / study builder for each project
- Web-based storyboard/tutorial for each project
- Co-developed projects with K-12 educators, musicians, researchers

**Key stats:**
- 1,900+ citizen science volunteers
- 18,000+ feature identifications
- Published paper: "Inverted radial Alfven continua" in Frontiers in Astronomy & Space Sciences, 2026

**Approach:** Work with audiences to co-develop projects. Use intake forms to capture goals/interests. Build tailored sonification interfaces rapidly using existing tools + AI. Remove barriers to entry for K-12 students and community members.

### Goal 3: STEM + Music Collaboration
Foster collaboration between STEM and music community using sonification of space data.

**Deliverables:**
- SONARA Sequencer: web-based beat generator using audified heliophysics data
- SONARA Synth: wavetable synthesizer loading audified satellite data as oscillator sources
- Curated sample packs from NASA datasets (researcher pipeline)
- Online space-sound music catalog
- Annual SONARA remix competitions
- Workshops at partner institutions connecting artists with scientists

**Evidence of impact:**
- 100M+ streams on prior commercial collaboration
- 113 countries reached
- 4+ university workshops

**Timeline:**
- Year 1 (2026): Prototypes, partnerships, pilot workshops
- Year 2 (2027): Public release of instruments, first remix competition, catalog launch
- Year 3 (2028): Scale workshops, curricular integration pilots
- Year 4 (2029): Iterate instruments, international expansion
- Year 5 (2030): Sustainability planning, summative evaluation

## Theory of Change

Interdisciplinary learning as disruption to siloed education. Key components:
- **Co-constructed learning**: Participants with range of expertise co-create artifacts
- **Innovative thinking**: Inviting variety of approaches fosters innovation beyond disciplinary boundaries
